Creating Buttons and Switches

The GPIO board is a bit different than the display and the audio amp... it is not a ready-made breakout board, but instead one we need to assemble. We need the following parts (also listed in the Hack Clock's v2 Wishlist):

  • A mini breadboard
  • A few push buttons
  • One or two N-channel MOSFETs (or a relay switch)
  • A 4.7k resistor for each MOSFET (probably won't need this for a relay)
  • Some short lengths of insulated wire
If you don't have a good stock of random resistors around, I would recommend ordering Sparkfun's resistor kit. It will save you on future projects!

Input (Buttons)

Let's wire up some buttons. The buttons will connect a general purpose input/output (GPIO) pin to a ground pin. Once the GPIO pin is connected to ground, our alarm clock will notice a button has been pressed and react likewise.
